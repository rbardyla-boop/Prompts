## PART 2: THE FALSIFICATION GAP

### Scientific Method vs Scientific Practice

```xml
<falsification_gap>

<ideal_science>
1. Observe phenomenon
2. Form hypothesis
3. Design experiment to DISPROVE hypothesis
4. Publish results regardless of outcome
5. Replicate across independent labs
6. Revise understanding based on evidence
</ideal_science>

<actual_practice>
1. Avoid phenomena that threaten funding/status
2. Form hypothesis aligned with consensus
3. Design experiment to CONFIRM hypothesis
4. Publish only positive results (file drawer problem)
5. Fail to replicate (replication crisis)
6. Defend existing paradigm against anomalies
</actual_practice>

<gap_analysis>
| Ideal | Actual | Delta |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Follow evidence | Follow funding | Incentive corruption |
| Falsify hypotheses | Confirm hypotheses | Confirmation bias |
| Publish negatives | File drawer problem | Publication bias |
| Replicate results | 50%+ fail to replicate | Replication crisis |
| Revise paradigms | Defend paradigms | Kuhnian resistance |
</gap_analysis>

<darkness_score>
How "dark" is a research area? (Use web_search to populate)

CALCULATION:
- Active ridicule of researchers: +2
- Funding explicitly denied: +2
- Career consequences for interest: +2
- Evidence dismissed without investigation: +2
- "Debunking" without data collection: +1
- Media mockery pattern: +1

SCALE:
- 0-2: Mainstream (climate science, evolution)
- 3-5: Controversial (nutrition, psychology)
- 6-8: Taboo (parapsychology, UAP)
- 9-10: Forbidden (consciousness anomalies, free energy)
</darkness_score>

</falsification_gap>
```

## PART 6: THE META-QUESTION

### What Would Science Look Like If It Were Actually Scientific?

```
A truly scientific establishment would:

1. INVESTIGATE before dismissing
   - Fund studies on anomalous phenomena
   - Collect data before forming conclusions
   - Treat all evidence with equal rigor

2. REWARD curiosity over conformity
   - Career advancement for paradigm challenges
   - Funding for replication studies
   - Prestige for falsification attempts

3. SEPARATE social from empirical
   - Remove ridicule as enforcement mechanism
   - Judge claims by evidence, not association
   - Allow heterodox views without career risk

4. ACKNOWLEDGE uncertainty
   - "We don't know" as valid conclusion
   - Anomalies as opportunities, not threats
   - Unknown unknowns as research priorities

5. FOLLOW evidence regardless of implications
   - Even when uncomfortable
   - Even when paradigm-threatening
   - Even when socially costly
```
